Comparative Analysis

I'm going to be comparing two sites that deal with measurements, as one a focus of my project is going to be measuring cars and illustrating how their height can cause deadly blind spots.

Carsized

Carsized.com is a great resource available to compare the dimensions of cars. The site allows users to browse from an extensive list of models and view the two cars side-by-side.

Dimensions

Dimensions.com is something I use a lot when 3D modelling in Blender. It has a seemingly endless list of articles describing the dimensions of everything from a Eames office chair (pictured above) to a Xenomorph.

Comparison

Carsized

  • Allows users to compare two objects side-by-side, providing height, width, and length measurements.
  • Allows users to observe from 3 different views: front, side, and rear.
  • Does not allow users to view fully 3D models of each car, just images.

Dimensions

  • Allows users to observe an object with both 2D CAD sketches and get a 360 view of a 3D model. The 3D aspect is not available for all articles.
  • Supplies detailed list of dimensions and other background information.
  • No way to compare items side-by-side with real proportions.
